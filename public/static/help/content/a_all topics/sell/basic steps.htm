Basic Steps

Here are some basic steps to get you started. For more details about the section, click the link at the end of the procedure.

Review Pending Orders

  1. On the main menu, click Order Management > Pending Orders.
  2. Click the order to see the order details.
  3. Click Show SpotThe television time bought market-by-market by an advertiser who wants to promote their company or brand. Allocations to see where the spots are scheduled to air.
  4. Review the dayparts, rates, cost, ratings/impressions.
  5. Go back to the Pending Orders screen.
  6. Click the arrow next to an order to see the order history.

For more information and detailed steps, click here.

Search for Orders

  1. On the main menu, click Order Management > Order Search.
  2. Enter information in the Search Order Listing section of the screen.
  3. Click Search. The results display in the table.

For more information and detailed steps, click here.

Review Avails

  1. On the main menu, click AvailsAvailabilities. Unsold spots available for sellers to sell to buyers or advertisers. Also refers to a seller's submission of program and rating estimates for advertising planning and buying..
  2. Search by AdvertiserOrganization or person who pays for the production, execution, and placement of an advertisement., AgencyAn organization acting as an agent for an advertiser devoted to developing and placing ads for the advertiser. The agency (advertising agency) represents an advertiser in the process of buying media. The agency typically takes a commission (commonly 15% or less) on the gross cost of each ad. The commission is typically paid as a discount on the invoice from the media partner to the agency. The agency bills the advertiser the gross amount of the invoice., and/or Avail Number.
  3. Click the arrow in the Avail # to see the avail Version History.
  4. Click the Avail # to see the avail details.
  5. Review the dayparts, goals, rates, ratings, and CPPCost Per Point. The buyer's cost to deliver a single rating point (one percent of the target audience within a specific geographic area). CPP is calculated by: Average Unit Cost ÷ Rating% or Totals Schedule Cost ÷ GRPs%./CPMCost Per Thousand. The buyer's cost to deliver a specific ad to 1,000 people or homes. CPM is calculated by: Media Cost ÷ Gross Impressions..
  6. If necessary, Edit BuyerA person authorized to place schedules for advertisers. The buyer purchases advertising space from a media partner. SpecsSpecifications. A document created by the station to communicate the format and delivery options for creative from an advertiser to a station. See also EPS, TIFF, JPG, PICT, PSD, AI, PDF, Alpha Channel. and Daypart Goals.
  7. Release to Buyer or Edit Avail.

For more information and detailed steps, click here.

Review Makegoods

  1. On the main menu, click Order Management > Pending Makegoods.
  2. Search by Offer Status, Offer Type, Agency, Advertiser, CampaignA specific advertising effort on behalf of a particular product or service which extends for a specific period of time. Campaigns link together multiple orders for specific advertisers., Agency Status, Station Status, and/or MG Transfers.
  3. Click the View Offer to see the makegood offer details.
  4. Click the Open link in View Order to see the original Order Detail.
