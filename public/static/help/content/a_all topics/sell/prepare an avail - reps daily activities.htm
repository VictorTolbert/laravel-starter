Prepare an Avail and Send it to an Agency

AvailsAvailabilities. Unsold spots available for sellers to sell to buyers or advertisers. Also refers to a seller's submission of program and rating estimates for advertising planning and buying. are delivered instantly through the Videa platform. Use the Avails screen in the Videa platform to add market competitive and to adjust rates and ratings.

  1. Open the Chrome browser (recommended) and go to ssp.videa.tv.
  2. In the Videa menu bar, click Avails.
  3. Click the Avails # for a NEW avail.
  4. At the bottom of the Avail ID screen, click Edit Avail.
  5. In the Avail ID Edit screen, build the avail worksheet in the following screens.

    • Click the Market Competitive tab to review the buyer's low and high CPPCost Per Point. The buyer's cost to deliver a single rating point (one percent of the target audience within a specific geographic area). CPP is calculated by: Average Unit Cost รท Rating% or Totals Schedule Cost รท GRPs%..
    • Click the BuyerA person authorized to place schedules for advertisers. The buyer purchases advertising space from a media partner. RatingsThe percentage of the entire audience (target universe) watching a specific media delivery vehicle (network, station, system, program, etc.) at a specific time. For example, if a target universe consists of 1,000,000 people 18-24 years old, and the content is exposed to 100,000 people 18-24 years old at 2:00PM, the rating among 18-24 year old people is 10.0. A rating should not be confused with share, which is a percentage of people using a media delivery vehicle at a particular time. tab to update the buyer ratings.
    • Click the Rates & Ratings tab to update the rates and ratings.

    Market Competitive screen: After discussions between the sales rep and buyer, enter the dollar amounts in the Market Competitive screen. This information also appears in the Need Low and Need High fields in the Buyer column in the Rates & Ratings screen (third image).

    Buyer Ratings screen: In the Buyer Rating column, enter the buyer's numbers from the UP report. This information also appears in the Buyer column in the Rates & Ratings screen (third image).

    Rates & Ratings screen: The Buyer column displays:

    • Competitive low and high information from the Market Competitive screen (first image).
    • Buyer ratings information from the Buyer Ratings screen (second image).

    The Videa column displays:

    • The Rating field based on ratings in the Price Guide.
    • The Need Goal (based on CPP), Need Low, and Need High fields based on the Market Competitive screen (first image).

    Important: In the Availed column:

    • Ratings come from agreed-to negotiations between the buyer and the rep.
    • The information from this screen goes to the buyer and validates the order.

  6. At the bottom of the Rates & Ratings screen, review the avail summary before sending it to the buyer. Click Save and Release.

  7. At the bottom of the Avail ID screen, click Release to Buyer.

  8. In the Release To Buyer window, click Submit.

    You can update the Expiration Date field; however, Videa recommends accepting the default date.

  9. In the Export Avail window, click CSV, XML, or Both XML and CSV.

  10. Go to the status bar at the bottom of the screen, click the file name arrowhead, and select Show in Folder.

  11. In the Downloads folder (or the folder you chose), right-click the XML and/or CSV file and click Send to > Mail recipient to email the files to the agency.
