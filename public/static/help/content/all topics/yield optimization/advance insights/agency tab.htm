Agency Tab

The AgencyAn organization acting as an agent for an advertiser devoted to developing and placing ads for the advertiser. The agency (advertising agency) represents an advertiser in the process of buying media. The agency typically takes a commission (commonly 15% or less) on the gross cost of each ad. The commission is typically paid as a discount on the invoice from the media partner to the agency. The agency bills the advertiser the gross amount of the invoice. tab displays shows the agencies using the Videa platform on the groups' stations. It also shows agency and advertiser performance across the groups’ stations.

Videa shows the agency as cards with high-level data points such as # (number) of Orders, Revenue, and Spots ordered through Videa.

The example shown below uses the AURAverage Unit Rate. The average cost per daypart for the spots aired during a campaign. filter.

Click an agency's name to see the values associated with that agency. Videa shows the advertiser roll-up with the corresponding stations receiving money from the agency and advertiser.

Each station is shown with the following order metrics:

  • # (number) of Orders
  • Revenue
  • AUR (or, CPPCost Per Point. The buyer's cost to deliver a single rating point (one percent of the target audience within a specific geographic area). CPP is calculated by: Average Unit Cost ÷ Rating% or Totals Schedule Cost ÷ GRPs%./CPMCost Per Thousand. The buyer's cost to deliver a specific ad to 1,000 people or homes. CPM is calculated by: Media Cost ÷ Gross Impressions.)
  • Spots
  • Market BudgetBuyer: The dollar amount to be spent in each market. Seller: The amount of money a broadcaster is expected to bill within a month, quarter, or year.
  • SharePercentage of households that are tuned to a specific program at a specific time.
