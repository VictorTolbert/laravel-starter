Price Guide Table

The Price Guide table allows stations to view the projected ratings and rates by daypart and quarter.

  • You can drill down into the daypart to see the programs.
  • You can drill down into the quarter to see the month and week for the daypart or program.

The table is divided into the station, Videa, On the Books, and Other Sources sections.

Field Description
Daypart

Daypart during which the campaign (or order) spots aired.

Station

The station rate that is submitted is used for all avails and is the rate seen by the buyer.

Field Description
RateThe price set for an individual piece of inventory. This price is generally a function of the demand for the inventory combined with its expected audience delivery. The rate for a program that will be submitted with avails. The default is the Videa rate. Overrides possible.
Rating/ImpressionsThe number of households (homes) or individuals exposed to an ad or a group of ads. The rating or number of impressions that will be submitted with avails. The default is the Videa rating/impressions. Overrides possible.
CPPCost Per Point. The buyer's cost to deliver a single rating point (one percent of the target audience within a specific geographic area). CPP is calculated by: Average Unit Cost ÷ Rating% or Totals Schedule Cost ÷ GRPs%./CPMCost Per Thousand. The buyer's cost to deliver a specific ad to 1,000 people or homes. CPM is calculated by: Media Cost ÷ Gross Impressions. The rate of a program divided by the rating/impressions of the program.

Videa

Field Description
Rate The suggested Videa rate for a program. Overrides not possible.
Rating/Impressions The rating/number of impressions sent with avails. The default is the Videa rating/impressions. Overrides not possible.
CPP/CPM The Videa rate of a program divided by the rating/impressions of the program.

On the Books

Field Description
Min Rate The lowest station rate that exists in a program at that point in time.
Max Rate The highest station rate that exists in a program at that point in time.
AURAverage Unit Rate. The average cost per daypart for the spots aired during a campaign. The program Average Unit Rate.
Sell OutThe amount of inventory that has sold out for a particular program. Often expressed as a percentage. For example, The Voice is at an 80% sell out level (meaning they have 20% left of available inventory to sell). % The percentage of spots sold in a program.
LY AUR Last year's Average Unit Rate.

Other Sources

SQAD Forecasting and planning tool to determine rate.
Mkt CPP A daypart or program's cost for each rating point, influenced by the current market conditions for that time period.
